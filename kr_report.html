Threat of Substitution

The most common payment method in South Korea is the credit cards. Credit card transactions contribute 75% of total transactions in Korea. Debit card transaction,21.1%, Pre-paid Transaction, a1%, Store Card Transaction, 1% as the following. Store card transaction was uncommon in Korea. However, in 04/2018 KEB Hana Card company just launched store card, and Shihan Card company also launched store card with One of Samsung's branch CJ. They have 100,000 customers in two months. The use of store card is increasing, and the trend is moving toward to store card in South Korea.

Forward-thinking governmental restructuring has resulted in the country scoring incredibly high on the World Bank’s scale measuring how easy it is to do business in different countries.
